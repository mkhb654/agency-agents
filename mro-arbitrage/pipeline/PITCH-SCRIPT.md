## When They Ask "What Are Airworthiness Directives?"

> "Airworthiness directives are regulations from the FAA that say — this part on this aircraft has a known issue, you need to replace it by this date or this flight hour.
>
> If you're a parts distributor or an MRO shop, you need to know about those because they create urgent demand spikes. A directive drops, suddenly everyone needs that part fast, and prices move.
>
> The dashboard surfaces those alerts in real time so you can see the demand signal before your competitors do, stock ahead of it, and charge appropriately.
>
> It's arbitrage intelligence for aerospace."

---

## When They Ask "How Does This Business Work?"

> "The money is in the aftermarket. Airlines and MRO shops have planes flying and parts break. They need replacements fast.
>
> The OEMs — Boeing, Airbus — they squeeze on new aircraft pricing. But the real margins are in the installed base. The 25,000 planes already flying.
>
> A distributor buys parts, stocks them, and sells to operators and MRO shops. The friction point is information. Thousands of part numbers, suppliers scattered globally, lead times that kill cash flow, and demand you can't predict until a maintenance event actually happens.
>
> That's where the dashboard wins. You're giving a distributor visibility into demand spikes before they happen — through airworthiness directives, failure rate data, and supply pricing. So they can stock ahead, charge premium, and not get caught flat-footed."

---

## When They Ask "What's The Margin Game?"

> "A distributor's margin is about three things. Buying cheap, stocking smart, and selling fast.
>
> Buying cheap means knowing what suppliers have in stock globally and negotiating before your competitors do. Right now that's manual — calling around, emailing, spreadsheets. The dashboard surfaces that instantly.
>
> Stocking smart means predicting demand. An airworthiness directive drops tomorrow affecting a thousand 737s. Every MRO needs that part within weeks. The distributor who stocks today charges 40% premium next month because everybody's desperate. The one who doesn't know till the phone rings — they're buying at retail to fill rush orders.
>
> Selling fast means having the part ready when the call comes. Your quote goes out in 4 minutes instead of 40. First one to respond wins the deal."

## Body Language / Delivery Notes

- **Don't read from slides.** The dashboard IS the presentation.
- **Lean back, not forward.** You're showing, not selling.
- **Let silence work.** After "Does that land for you?" — wait. Don't fill the gap.
- **Match their energy.** Raul is technical — go deep. Bill is CEO — go high-level. Moulay is relationship — go personal.
- **When they lean in and start asking questions — stop talking.** They're sold. Answer their questions. Don't keep pitching.
